I guess we're about due for an update!  First off, I want anyone reading this to be aware that it's end of year, so CaringBridge has plenty of pop-ups and ads that ask for money for their platform - FYI, that ain't me and I am unable to remove them!  Please just ignore and carry on. :) 

The overall vibe for our family at the moment is ironically not far off from last year's vibe around this time with a post containing some lyrics from a Counting Crows song, "A Long December". 

_"It's been a long December and there's reason to believe, 
maybe this year will be better than the last"_

I've been asked plenty about Lyla lately, and although the exact words aren't said, I think the underlying question and concern is consistent amongst everyone - "Did the second surgery work???"  Well... it's been a bit of a mixed bag.  Let me try to compartmentalize it a bit.

**Some good**: Her behavior, clarity of thought, sense of humor, and fluency all received a pretty major upgrade post-surgery.  The doctors said it would be within the realm of normal to see some seizure activity while her brain heals over the 8-10 week period following surgery (Dec. 19th marked 8 weeks while this Friday will mark 10 full weeks), so we've simply been monitoring it and trying not to over-analyze.  I can't even begin to explain how impossible that is to say/type when her seizure activity ebbs and flows the way it does.  She had nearly FOUR days of zero seizure activity in the days leading up to Christmas, and that hasn't happened in over 2 years!

**Some bad**: Overall, however, her seizure activity has remained pretty much the same.  She's starting to have some right side motor activity during seizures and that's an indication of Rasmussen's.  In addition to an average of 8 or so auras per day and about 1 focal per day, she has also had a couple really intense seizures in December while at school and her rescue med didn't pull her out, so ambulance was called for both.  Losing trust in our rescue med is definitely a new scary element to navigate.  We now have to keep the nearest hospital in mind anytime we go anywhere.  (e.g. - "Ok, we need to make a run to Columbus.  There's a decent hospital within 5 minutes of where we'll be, so we only need to be mindful of the trip to/from and if we move to another area of Columbus.  Ok, now we're going from Polaris to Easton.  What hospital is closest to Easton?  And so on...)

**Some sweet**: Aside from the good and bad, there have been some really sweet moments too.  [We got her a new bike for Christmas](https://www.facebook.com/share/v/1F5X5CoACu/), and boy was she floored!  After the initial shock and excitement wore off a bit, she wanted to go outside and give it a test ride.  However, she had an internal recognition that she wasn't sure if she'd be able to do it or if she'd even remember how to ride a bike.  She asked us shyly if we could teach her how to ride a bike again.  Oh Lyla... YOU BETTER BELIEVE IT, SWEETHEART!  I will teach you to ride your bike all over again next year, the year after that, a month after that, whatever!!  Well as it turned out, she was just fine - she got on that bike and rode it around the driveway without any problems whatsoever.  

Another moment that kind of bridges the sweet/heart-wrenching categories came last night while cuddling on the couch right before bed time.  She said to me quietly, "Daddy, I just wish I was a normal kid".  Oof.  Not ready to unpack the meaning of "normal kid", but we both knew what she meant.  She wants the seizures to go away, she wants her emotions to be normal again, to understand math fully again, to be at the top of her class in reading again, to do a cartwheel, to play softball, to jump on the trampoline... just... normal kid stuff.  I thank God for giving me words in that moment, so I can't take credit for what was said after: 

"Lyla... the way you live your life is unlike any young person I've met.  You're teaching this old dog (your own Dad!) new tricks.  You are serious about your faith, and you take every opportunity you can to show the love of God to others amidst your own pain and heartbreak.  You have the main things right.  Keep chasing after God and showing His great love to others - that's literally the only stuff that matters.  Yes, I also wish you didn't have seizures.  But maybe God knew you were the only 10 year old kiddo that could handle this amount of tragedy and still point others to God?  God loves your heart and is using it!  In any case... Keep doing what you're doing, girl.  You've got the main things right!"  

And as a side note... I'm just going to keep writing it all down.  

**Some heart-wrenching**: We had a follow up with Dr. Bingaman on Dec. 9th, and the amount of continued seizure activity we've been seeing is plenty more than expected.  A hemispherectomy is on the table as the next step if things don't change.  We sat down for lunch (just the 3 of us) right after that appointment and it was a pretty emotional time for Kayla and I.  Looking at her right now vs. imagining how she'd potentially look/talk/walk/etc. in the future if a hemispherectomy is done.  Lyla caught on to the fact that we were distraught and in despair, so she calmly put her hand on Kayla's shoulder and said this to her (I had Kayla write it down verbatim and text it to me so we never forget):

_"Believe in the Lord. Whatever comes, Whatever is going to happen, believe in Him mom._

_Mom, I can do this. I can go through this. I have you and daddy by my side and I have God, Jesus, and Holy Spirit with me. They will never leave me. Don’t worry. Believe. _

_We’ve got this. WE’VE got this!"_

I can't imagine that she fully understands what is involved with a hemispherectomy, but she HAS been through plenty already.  Given her own history, she was welcoming more and trying to encourage her own parents and point us back to Jesus.  Well done, kiddo! 

She was also drawn to some artwork near where we ate lunch that contained some scripture references:

_Proverbs 31:25 - "She is clothed with strength and dignity; she can laugh at the days to come"_

_Psalm 121:1-2 - "I lift up my eyes to the mountains - where does my help come from?  My help comes from the Lord, the Maker of heaven and earth."_

On another note... She turned 10 on December 27th and had an epic day of fun in Columbus at Pins and Build-a-Bear prior to coming back to the area for dinner at Texas Roadhouse.  She's been DYING to be the birthday girl for their whole "Saddle up" Shpiel.  She also had 14 auras and 3 focals that day, so... a mixed bag, I guess? 

One of her birthday presents this year was a new set of bluetooth headphones.  She immediately connected them to Kayla's phone and began perusing Spotify.  If you know her, you know she loves to listen to and sing anything worship.  Well... she discovered a song on her own and came over to show me.

_"Dad, listen to this song I just found - I really like the words!!"_

I'm just gonna leave this here - the entirety of this gem.

**Final Thoughts**:
Never in a million years.  Never would I have guessed we'd be here.  I'm not talking about another potential surgery.  I'm talking about where my family stands in faith.  Rest assured, we are not strong - God is taking care of that part.  God is gracious enough to show us these moments of beauty to keep us going and pressing onward.  The unbelievable part?  That God is somehow using this ordinary, kinda goofy, spiritually broken (and many times emotionally/mentally) Klopfenstein family and furthering His kingdom as we continue to learn and grow.

**Prayer**:
God, I pray that our family remains firm and unified in You.  More tragedy might be ahead, and if that's the case, then so be it.  Help us all find our joy in You as well as showing Your love to others.
