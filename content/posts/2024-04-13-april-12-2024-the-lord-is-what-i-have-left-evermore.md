**Family Update**:
We didn't realize our basement would get flooded as well.  But yep - that happened too.  The basement: the one place in the house that didn't seem to get hit so bad with smoke or fire damage.  

We were walking through the house yesterday with our adjuster as well as the repair/cleaning folks (all wonderful people to work with, by the way), and eventually made our way to the basement.  I was hopeful that it'd be a mostly untouched area from the fire and smoke.  It was, but also had some flooding anywhere from drops of water (high areas) to about 3 inches (low areas).  I gotta admit, I had a breaking point.  Just had to take a few minutes to myself and just hard cry for a bit.  A solid mix of anger and fatigue. I wanted to write yesterday, but I need to admit that I just flat out couldn't.  It probably would've just resulted in an all-caps single word post: "WHHHYYYYYY???!?!?!?!?!"

Nonetheless, this is where the rubber meets the road in our faith.  With each new tragedy (well... they're tragedies for US anyway), we have a choice to make.  Is it our outcome that determines our faith?  Or is God still present in this moment?  Is he continuing to show His presence in the wreckage?  

Unfortunately, this post isn't ending with answers, revelations, or wisdom.  Just a pleading with God that He uses our continued misfortune for His glory.  I think it's appropriate because the stories we're all journeying through won't always end each evening in a happy and uplifting tone.  Sometimes it's full of questions, sorrow, and fatigue. But foundational to our faith, I believe God transcends all of that.  He understands pain a whole lot better than I do.  My "pain" pales in comparison.

I keep going back to the lyrics that were put on my heart - "Tear it all down... when my faith's in outcomes." and "Here in Your presence, my purpose is found."  

**Relevant Scripture**:
Lamentations 3:24 (ICB) - I say to myself, "The Lord is what I have left.  So I have hope."

**Prayer:**
God, keep tearing down the places of my life, both figurative and literal, where I put my faith in that isn't You.  Help me to recognize that Your plan for me isn't my job, my health, my home, etc.  It's Your presence.  Use even my times of weakness to show Your extreme love to others. I love You. Amen
