We're home!!  It's truly unbelievable that we're sitting here at home a mere FOUR DAYS after brain surgery.  [Here's a quick message from Lyla herself](https://www.facebook.com/164901175/videos/365705169558391/) 

On the way home, we were blessed with an unrelenting sunset for what seemed like the entirety of the trip back south on 71.  For the first bit, it was all about the awe and beauty of God and His nature - just stunning! 

Then... I got to thinking about the word "Sunset" as it applies to my job and technology in general.  It's usually meant as a bad thing.  "X product is being sunset" means that it's no longer being built out, developed, or supported - it's going away.  I couldn't help but wonder if this sunset was a bit symbolic.  I realize we're not in the clear yet, but how fitting it would be as we're driving home after brain surgery to a beautiful sunset, that we're potentially "sunsetting" or saying goodbye to the last 9 months. 

There's a new horizon coming, a new season coming.  It will have its own set of challenges for sure, but the thought of maybe, MAYBE being done with this season brought tears to my eyes.  Pretty sure I'd be just fine never hearing the word "seizure" ever again.  :) 

Couldn't help then but think through some of things that are being "sunset" in our lives if this has cured Lyla (who, by the way, has been seizure-free for four days straight now!!).  What is gone as we enter a new season?  What is yet to come?  And heck, what even happened up there in Cleveland??  It all happened so fast that I barely had time to process any of it (which was probably a good thing).  

There are plenty of stories that don't have a positive outcome.  So it begs the question... Why did God let US have a positive outcome?  I promise it's nothing we did, and even more so... I promise that I don't have an answer to this.  But it brought up a couple more questions as I listened to Andrew Word's song, "Unfailing".  Is God any less loving, any less kind, any less good had He chosen a different path for Lyla?  Did I need a positive outcome with Lyla's surgery to determine my faith?  And if I need positive outcomes at all times to make God good... Who is the main character here??  

**Somehow, God is good ALL the time... and it's not because of my personal outcomes.**
