Guys... I'm slacking.  It's been a while again.  There are more ups and downs for Lyla, while not much has changed with the house.

**Lyla's update**:
This past week was ROCKIN up until about an hour ago.  She had 1 focal and 2 auras TOTAL in the past 8 days.  Prior to that, she had been maybe averaging somewhere around 1-2 focals per day.  She had a small focal and an aura this morning, so... we're good for the day then, right? 😬

She went into a small 20 second focal, came out, recovered quickly, then a few minutes later rattled off a series of who knows how many focals back to back.  It was hard to tell if she was coming out of it or not, but her eyes would soften and make eye contact, then she'd go right back into seizing.  We held off on the rescue med because she kept "coming out of it", but I think we're  both now wondering if that was the right call or not.  All in all, the seizing stopped finally after about 9 minutes.  She was post-ictal for at least another 10 (That's the period of time where she's out of the seizure, but still confused or doesn't "have her words back yet" as we like to say).  Ugh, what a terrible feeling.  Lots of worry setting in with school starting up again in two weeks.  The school has already been in contact with us to help ease fears and care for her in the best possible way.  Seriously... MAJOR THANK YOU to Mike, Tara, and many many others including friends and family for thinking of Lyla (and us!) and reaching out.  Can't tell you just how far reaching that has been for us.  Because it's worrisome, ya know?  Worrisome when we have no idea of knowing when the next seizure will be, or how big it's going to be.  

I did a quick search on "Worry" via the bible app, and stumbled across Matthew 6:25-34 which contains commonly quoted passages like: 

"Look at the birds in the air. They don’t plant or harvest or store food in barns. But your heavenly Father feeds the birds. And you know that you are worth much more than the birds."

and "You cannot add any time to your life by worrying about it."

and "And why do you worry about clothes? Look at the flowers in the field. See how they grow. They don’t work or make clothes for themselves."

but something really stuck out to me in verse 33: "The thing you should want most is God’s kingdom and doing what God wants. Then all these other things you need will be given to you."  

Focus on doing what God wants.  I think I could probably frame that and learn from it daily for the rest of my life.  Am I that selfish that I need reminded constantly that I'm not the main character in this story?  Focus on doing what God wants... Focus on doing what God wants... 

We're heading up to Cleveland this week for a few follow up appointments with our beloved team up there (Dr. Bingaman, Dr. Pestana-Knight, and more!).  She'll have an EEG to see if and how the brain has changed since surgery.  Hopefully we'll get some decent answers, and hopefully we'll have more and more weeks like this one (sans tonight's activities!).

**Micah**:
Big brother.  Gotta give this kid some recognition.  He has grown up this past year way more than an 11-year-old should have to.  We've struggled to establish some balance between letting him just be a kid (shielding him) and needing him to chip in as a teammate and help in some of the tense moments.

It's unfair.  

I hate that he has to see some of the stuff he sees.  I hate that he has played the role of "First responder" (more than once!).  He's not a parent, but he certainly has a role.  He's grown in height this year by probably 100 inches, but I think his character has grown even more.  I'm proud of him.  I'm proud of the friends he's surrounded himself with.  I'm proud of how he handles himself mentally and emotionally on the field as an athlete amidst turmoil (turmoil on the the field too, but referring mostly to off-field).  I make sure now to tell him when he does something a grown man would do (or ought to do, rather) without any prompting.  

Speaking of his friends... boy oh boy am I impressed.  Not just the quality of friends, but the quality of their families as well.  When we moved to Ontario, we prayed very specifically for his group of friends, and God has certainly placed the right people at the right times.  These kids are flat out awesome, and the parents/siblings are equally as cool.  Thanks for welcoming us in. :) 

So with one prayer answered for Micah's circle of friends, and another big one in progress for Lyla's health, the song on repeat is "Anything is Possible"
