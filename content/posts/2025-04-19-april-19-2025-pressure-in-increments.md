Wow.  It's been two months since the last update??  How is that possible?  Please forgive me as there's still a lot on our plate! :) 

Lyla's update: 

IVIg - We're still going strong here, but she just had her fourth infusion and it doesn't seem to be changing much, unfortunately.  If anything, number of seizures and intensity of seizures seem to be ramping up again. She's had quite a few intense seizures at school over the last several weeks, and I feel like it's at a point where I'm going to need to write up a letter to her class both thanking them for their maturity and support and friendship, but also apologizing to them for forcing them to grow up and mature quickly in ways no parent desires for their kiddos.  But man, why stop with a letter to her class?  Why not a much deserved letter to her teachers, Ms. Puckett and Ms. Jill??  Why not a letter to Ontario Youth Sports and other parents that just seem to have an understanding about Lyla and what she's able to do.  Sounds like I've got some work to do here!  

Back to some health stuff, we had a follow up appointment with the team from Mayo last week, and it appears as though the next logical step would be an sEEG - an invasive EEG that involves surgery to place nodes into her brain.  We've never seen her seizure activity from deep inside the brain, so perhaps that would provide new information.  It certainly also raises our level of concern any time they're literally touching her brain.  Lots of thoughts there, but I'll save that for another time. We haven't decided if or how we're moving forward with this procedure, but we are talking with the Neurosurgeon from Mayo on Wednesday to go through the details.  We have also talked with Cleveland recently and they seem to be of the same mindset - that we're on a collision course with a second surgery of some sort.  

All of this brings to mind a song from a band I really like - "Constance" by Spiritbox (100% Definitely NOT a Christian band as evidenced by several of their songs, but an incredible rock band nonetheless! :D )  They wrote this song about a band member's grandmother who was battling dementia.  I feel this song deeply anytime Lyla has a rough day mentally and I'm able to compare it to her from a year or two ago.

_"The pressure in increments
Like a slow moving coup
Memories dissident when I am holding you."

_It's tough watching someone you love deeply regress.  No wait... that's not nearly strong enough wording.  It's soul crushing.  I'd rather get hit by a bus.

However, I continue to hold out some level of hope that God's plan might involve some form of healing and restoration for her.  In the same breath, I need to remind myself that we don't need healing to know and see the goodness of God.  Lyla remains firmly in love with "God, Jesus, and Holy Spirit", and there is no greater joy as a parent than understanding your child's commitment to Christ.   

I'm trying to be present and experience more "little moments" with her and with our family.  Even today, driving in the car together, we heard this worship song "[Confident](https://open.spotify.com/track/6pjhWRapvpyBv6bY59QACN?si=0cc45fd2ca8b422e)", and Lyla started singing it.  Kay joined in after a bit, then myself, and even Micah.  It was a two part blessing to me: 1) The fact that Lyla started it was beautiful to me. 2) Singing those specific words together as some sort of anthem for our family choked me up several times.  Here are my favorite parts:

_"...I remain confident 
I will see the goodness of the Lord.
And this hope will never fade
It will hold my soul within the trial....

....Give me faith to trust that You will
Take me right through it
When the mountains stand before me
And my confidence is shaken..."_

House update:

We're getting super close!  We're set to move back in within the next month to month and a half from now.  It's really neat to see restoration progress.  Quick tangent - with tomorrow being Easter, I went back to an instrumental song I had written a few years ago about the book of Luke and life of Jesus.  I hesitate saying anything for fear of looking like some sort of self-promotion, but I promise you... I'm my own harshest critic, and it's uncomfortable at best to share things I've written from the heart musically.  Everything was written very intentionally where specific instruments represent people or elements in the life of Jesus, then there is "death" and "resurrection" sections of the song, but the way it ends is what humbled me and brought me to tears (hey, what else is new, right??) It was initially created as a Christmas tune, not an Easter tune, and the end was supposed to have this "Home for the holidays" feel.  I tried to capture that by literally recording the crackling fire in the fireplace at the crispy house and recording the piano part of the song on my old, out of tune piano from across the room.  Listening back to it this time (after not hearing it for a year or so) was really emotional.  The fire.  The piano that we lost in the fire.  "Home for the holidays" - what is home?  We weren't there for the holidays this year.  Just lots of thoughts and emotions and processing there.  If you're interested in listening, you can find it here along with timestamps and roadmap of the song with descriptions: [https://soundcloud.com/lklopfenstein/luke?in=lklopfenstein/sets/instrumentals](https://soundcloud.com/lklopfenstein/luke?in=lklopfenstein/sets/instrumentals)

Do me a favor: if you like it, don't comment on it.  If you hate it, don't comment on it.  Please just take it for what it is - honest and sincere worship of the one true God.
