**The Lyla Update**:
Lyla seems to be doing pretty well now.  She was at Cleveland Clinic in the Pediatric EMU (Epilepsy Monitoring Unit) from Easter (March 31st) until Wednesday, April 3rd.  She DID have two focal seizures on Wednesday - one that was caught on the video EEG, and one once she got home.  They doubled one of her meds, and took her off the Fycompa, which we're just going to hope is the new "secret cocktail" of meds that works well for her.  Since then, she's had zero focal seizures and two auras - one on Friday and one today.  I'll take it!!!

**The House Update**:
Admittedly, there's a ton more to share on this end since it's still so new and fresh, and I haven't really shared much yet here.  So the story goes something like this... 

Micah and I were heading up to Cleveland to visit the girls in the hospital for a little bit, but were worried about the coming storms (and threats of tornados), so we made a plan.  We have a sump pump, but if the power goes out, our basement is in danger of flooding.  So our plan was, if we get a notification on our phones that our wifi went down, we'd call up my Dad, and he'd run over and connect our sump pump to the power station I had been charging on our kitchen counter.  

Everything had gone really well until Micah and I were on our way back.  We stopped for gas at the Lodi exit about halfway to home when I got a text from Kay that wifi had gone down, so I immediately gave Dad a call.  He zipped over to the house, but when he tried to enter through the garage, he was met with a cloud of smoke and some orange embers on the ground.  He immediately called 911 and the fire trucks were there in about 4 minutes.  By the time Micah and I arrived, the scene was pretty wild and surreal - 3 or 4 fire trucks, lots of flashing lights, spotlight on the house, etc.  A fireman had us come around to the back to see what had happened - complete and utter destruction of our kitchen.  Like a war zone.  

Over the next few minutes of staring blankly from the back patio while the firemen went to work, several friends and family members began to show up - Cody, Tim, Chet (also my insurance agent, go figure!), Adam, Ben, Kevin, and more.  They showed up in support and it meant the world to me.  Chet mentioned in passing that as a silver lining, I'm gonna be happy with my insurance - I just need to cover the deductible of $1,000.  

It's such a strange and surreal feeling to look around at the rubble and see remnants of OUR stuff poking through the ashes - burnt art projects from the kiddos, shards of a mug I use often, an old budgeting planner, etc.  One of the firemen asked if there was anything of value, and I was like... "Well... I mean... my coffeemaker was over there, Google home speaker over there... " and as I was saying this out loud I'm thinking "No, actually.  You could probably just pitch all this stuff."  It was as if I was struck with this realization that God provided for us yet again in such a unique way in all this mess.  None of us were home - My people.  My family.  I was hit with a sudden recognition of what's priority - what's truly important to me. 

And it sure wasn't my prized coffee maker.  

But then I remembered (OOOHHH NOOOOOO) that there was a little cardboard gift box that contained some things that various folks had gifted us (cash/gift cards) over the past several months.  The cash... I have no explanation of why it hadn't made it to the bank yet, but the dread I felt when I looked up at the cupboard that contained the box and saw that it WASN'T EVEN THERE... my heart just dropped.  All those generous friends/family/strangers/etc.... I'm that bad of a steward of what was provided to me, and now it's gone.  The cupboard had completely burnt to ashes, and the ashes were all over the counter.  

The firemen were shoveling heaps of "The hot stuff" onto my back porch (Someone took a picture just for reference), and as I was frantically eyeing the counter for that darn box, Dad yells out - "Micah found the box!!"  Also, look closely and you'll see that box in the pile of still smoldering rubble.  I had to open it up to count out the money for the fire report - $1,020.  Enough to cover the deductible and perhaps some lunch.  And to boot - the box had soot on top and some mud on the side in a few spots, but absolutely not a single burn mark.  I guess I didn't realize that it was one of those new fireproof cardboard boxes.  😂

Skipping to a few days later for a sec... we're living at my mother-in-law's house for the interim, and some good friends of ours stopped by to "give us something".  It was a card with another amount to cover the deductible.  Instant tears.  Woah... God is showing kindness to us in a two-fold means.  Almost as if to say...

"**Lee, chill.  If that box wouldn't have been found, I would've still had this covered!"  **

So now here we sit... with a double portion of blessing.  Now I'm looking side to side and wide-eyed thinking, "Okayyyy, so that must mean I'll be needing a second deductible for some reason."  Well, I'm trying to look for that reason now.  Maybe something weird is about to happen with insurance.  Maybe something is going to be valued all wrong.  Maybe someone else I come across will have an even GREATER need?  Who knows, but I'm trying to live more open-handed in that sense.  

Okay, so back to the night of the fire, one thing that wasn't consumed in the blaze is a wall decoration in the kitchen that has a place for our "Scripture of the week".   Here's what was there:

[Lamentations 3:22-24](https://www.bible.com/bible/1359/LAM.3.22-24)
(ICB Version) 'The Lord’s love never ends. His mercies never stop. They are new every morning. Lord, your loyalty is great. I say to myself, “The Lord is what I have left. So I have hope.”'

Although... it looked a bit different.  Some singe marks, some soot, something that looked like black tears rolling off the bottom edge.  It's dark, yet makes me tear up when I see it - "The Lord is what I have left.  So I have hope".

Another bit of beauty is that the fire spread to the living room (right past the scripture of the week?) and caught our bin of blankets and curtain on fire.  We assume the firefighters caught it at that point, because that would've spread to the rest of the house FAST from there.  I don't know what to do with this bit yet, but the fire turned on our fake candle.  Annndddd.... we can't turn it off.  Like... it just won't turn off.   Our friends saw/heard about that and sent us this bit:

[John 1:5](https://www.bible.com/bible/111/JHN.1.5)
'The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it.'

So simple and so powerful.  

It was still on last night and the only light in the house.  

Finally, today at church was awesome.  New meaning was brought to the song "Firm Foundation" for me when it comes to that lyric, "Rain came, wind blew, My house was built on You."  It was already meaningful in a figurative sense with our family (our house) coming together around all the stuff with Lyla.  But now, in a quite the literal sense, I'm proclaiming, "MY HOUSE IS BUILT ON YOU!!"  It really makes me look forward to the upcoming period of restoration for our house and all the parallels I'm about to openly weep over (don't forget, I weep for fun now). 

Anyway, this is a lengthy one.  My apologies.  Here's the TL;DR version (Too long; didn't read): Lyla's doing well, house caught on fire - cool stuff happened anyway. 

**Final thoughts**:

**I'm finding that God doesn't keep us from misfortune.  Rather, He shows up in the very wreckage of it.  His presence (not hype, not false teachings, etc., but rather actual, genuine presence) is so compelling that somehow... I'm inviting more.  **

If it means more of the beauty of God that we've been seeing, I'm in for more.  Bring your worst!!  (Ok, maybe not the worst 😬 - newbie here... still learning.)  From my recent experience, it seems appropriate to invite others to follow Jesus by saying, "Hey... you probably don't want to do this.  Yes, it'll change your life forever.  Yes, it's 110% worth it.  But... if you can't withstand enormous pain, this might not be for you.  It's certainly not for the faint of heart!" 

You've got to count the cost, and I have zero regrets.
