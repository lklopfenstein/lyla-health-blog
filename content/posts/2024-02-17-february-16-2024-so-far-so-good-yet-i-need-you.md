Some pretty darn big steps today!! 

**Positives**:

- She is supposed to step down from the PICU to a regular EMU (Epilepsy Monitoring Unit) room tonight. 
- Catheter came out
- Arterial line came out 
- She ate decently well during the day
- They took her off the major drip med after surgery
- They're already talking about weening her off her daily seizure meds (She's currently on large doses of 4 different daily meds)
- She's being her sweet self again.  I'm pretty sure we haven't seen that in months.
- She's TALKING.  She's holding some great conversation with us.  She's joking with us.  She's smiling.
- Best of all... It's been since 1pm yesterday since she came out of surgery.  ZERO seizures so far.  No auras, no focals, no generalized, nada, nothing.  I wish you all could've watched me type that.  It was with... umm... vigor.  ha!  Now, it's going to be a long road because Dr. Bingaman told us that we can't really consider ourselves in the "clear" until we've gone 5 years without a seizure.  But hey, I'll take a single day - it's gotta start somewhere and we haven't had a single seizure-free day since September 2023. 

So... You're telling me she just had brain surgery less than 24 hours ago??  And she's acting like [THIS](https://www.facebook.com/lklopfenstein/posts/pfbid0FmjVMFHHLTcRm1LXfPXYzE8iefyvX4JaaY4hygX42Hi1hVSg4RgijE9bJ1qdCLyTl?notif_id=1708141130882048&notif_t=feedback_reaction_generic&ref=notif)??  If you didn't already know, you'd never know.  Craziest thing. 

**Negatives:**

- Still dealing with nausea, and that got in the way of dinner tonight.  We assume it's due to the crazy amount of meds she's still on.

**My Takeaway**:

It was an unbelievably and overwhelmingly positive day.  Yet, I mentioned in a prior post that it's easy for me to turn to God in the tough times - when there's nowhere else to turn.  What about when life is going swimmingly?? 

For a bit of context, I had unfortunately spent around 30 years of my life acting as if I was firm in my faith when, in reality, I was simply an occasional fan of Jesus, the man.  My life was great, and I didn't _need_ Him.  I didn't _really depend_ on Him.  At least... not until about a year and a half ago when an unrelated experience broke us down to a point where there was nothing left to do but try complete and utter dependence upon Him.  I'm here to tell you that the decision to double down on God has made all the difference in my life, my marriage, my family, etc.  I'm able to admit now that I'm going to be a work in progress for the rest of my life.

**...And that spiritual brokenness is important because the thorn that broke us and plagued our recent past is the very thorn that prepared us to go through this war zone for the last nine months.**

Don't count out the tough days, months, and years, friends and fam - I promise they have value, even when it feels like that's impossible. ❤️
