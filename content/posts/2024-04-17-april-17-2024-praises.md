**Lyla's update**:

We've got quite a few updates to share that are all great!  

- **Return to school**.  She's been SO pumped about this.  Finally, a return to normal.  A return to recess, friends, schoolwork, cartwheels at recess, lunchtime conversations, etc.  She's loving every bit of it.
- **Interest in softball**.  She wanted to play, so we're playing!  She asks to play catch, field grounders, and bat just about every day.  She's loving it, and she'll have her first team practice on Thursday (She couldn't make the first one due to not feeling well).
- **Baptism**.  It's happening, folks!!  It was supposed to happen back in January, I believe?  Sorry, it's all been a bit of a blur.  She missed it due to one of her hospital stays and was devastated.  At our church, each person that chooses to be baptized writes out a brief version of their story of coming to faith and reasoning why they want to be baptized.  They then turn this into a short video.  Back in January, she had created a video, but 1) it took forever to create because they were trying to record it between seizures, and 2) The story is... different now.  The testimony has changed quite a bit.  She records the new version of her video today, and I can't wait to share it with you all!  Sunday, April 21st at Core Community Church in Shelby, OH @ 10:45am service for whoever is interested - she will be one of several being baptized.  Consider this an invite to show up if you've been invested in her in any way throughout her journey - be it prayer, cards, meals, or even just some encouraging words.
- **Visit to Newville Church this past Sunday**. We had an opportunity to visit my parent's church, and what a wonderful little visit it was!!  They've been in fervent prayer over Lyla as an entire church body for almost a year now with most never having met her before.  [She was invited up on stage and even had a quick opportunity to say something](https://www.facebook.com/164901175/videos/303999909235881/).  We had opportunities to express our deepest and most sincere "Thank you's", meet some lovely new friends, and even show a couple people our newfound ["Fireproof cardboard box"](https://www.facebook.com/164901175/videos/761477292717393/) (It initially came from this church).  We linked arms (figuratively) and worshiped in a way that is different from what we're used to (hymns), and it was so incredibly beautiful.

Quick side note: I'd love to encourage everyone to try worship in a way that is different from what you're used to - if it's difficult at first, wrestle with why!  I've spent too many years focused on musical aspects of worship that simply don't matter to God (genre, songwriting, musicality, volume, etc.). **The fact is, we're all broken.  None of us have the perfect words, chords, melodies/harmonies to say to God in worship.  And quite frankly, as stupid as it sounds, I've come to realize that worship isn't FOR me... it's for God.  I think God honors the effort, the try, the meaningful attempt. ** I'll admit that what I used to do was cross my arms in silence when worship wasn't "going my way" (that'll show 'em! 😄).  It would've been much more fruitful all along to put aside any personal preferences and join the unified voice of the church body, whatever that may look or sound like.

**Family update**:

- **Moving in today**.  Family has been so kind and generous to us in offering places to stay (both short and long term).  We feel so incredibly lucky to have the support system we have.  But alas, it is time to move on from where we've been staying short term, and move into the long term option that we chose after days of prayerfully working through what option makes the most sense for our family.  We may be out of our neighborhood, but we are able to stay in Ontario, so we're close! Thanks to everyone for the leads!!!
- **Wheels are in motion**.  Everyone we've worked with so far at Westfield, ServPro, InServio, Dream Huge, ALE Solutions, and more... they've been such lovely people.  God is choosing to bless us with some really fun and caring people along the way - here's hoping our humble little fam can show God's love to everyone we encounter.
