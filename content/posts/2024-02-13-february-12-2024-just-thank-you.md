Just a quick little message here to tell anyone and everyone who has committed to prayer for our family, shared encouraging words privately, publicly, whatever, given cards, gifts, anything... 

From the bottom of the Klops' hearts... THANK YOU.  We want to recognize the care and love that everyone is showing and has shown to us, and the boost of morale and security that it has brought and continues to bring has been undeniable.  

Lyla has brain surgery on Thursday.  

We see this potential light at the end of the tunnel (I guess with complications, it could be the beginning of another journey, but hey, that's a post for another time).  

Or perhaps a better metaphor is that for nine months, the four of us have been pushing a giant boulder up a mountain.  With each encouraging word or prayer prayed over us, it's as if we have another person jumping in to help push from behind.  I'm looking back a bit and reflecting tonight and realizing that we are potentially 100 feet from the summit of this nightmare-ish 14,000 ft. mountain.  We're weak, beaten, and bruised. However, if I turn around for a second, I see this support system from all areas of our lives that has turned into an incredibly diverse (and I mean EVERY age, sex, race, religion, location, language) and wonderful army willing to take a little bruising and beating on our behalf.  Some of y'all are the closest of friends and family.  Some of y'all I have literally never met.  In any case, THAT is what the love of Jesus looks like.  Seeing it play out like this for my daughter and family is motivating, invigorating, etc.  

I'm now ready and fully prepared to run through a brick wall if need be.

All of a sudden, this boulder and this mountain don't seem quite so daunting.  
Alright Kayla, Micah, and Lyla... we can do this.  
Let's finish.
