The board decided that the best plan is to have Lyla get an sEEG as the next step. While at first this is not what we were hoping for, we trust their recommendation. Right now it is hard to tell what areas of the brain are part of the malformation and what areas are just swollen due to seizure activity. It is a larger area they are looking at. With this area being vital to her memory and learning they want to be as precise as possible without taking out more than they need to... and that requires doing the sEEG. 

So our next steps are to meet with the neurosurgeon and our epileptologist. At this appointment we can ask all our questions and we will schedule the sEEG. The sEEG itself will be a 5-6 hour surgery with most likely a 7-10 day hospital stay. 

The final surgery will be a resection of part of her left temporal lobe (mostly the hippocampus area) but the SEEG will give us more clarity as to the exact area and how much of the brain they will need to take out. This surgery will be around 7 weeks after the sEEG

This is all a lot and we are doing a lot of processing. I know this is mostly facts but I don't know that I can even express my emotions right now. I love you all. ❤️ .
