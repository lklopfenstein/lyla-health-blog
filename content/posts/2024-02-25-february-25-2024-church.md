All is still well at home.  It has now been ten full days since her surgery, and still not a single seizure!  Now, any time someone asks about all of this, I can't help but respond with "I feel like I can breathe".  There's this acute awareness of just how much time was spent having our shoulder tapped, hearing "I'm getting that belly feeling", documenting the start of a seizure, documenting if it turns into a focal or not, writing out a quick description and length, trying to tend to something else for a minute or two, then starting that process all over again.  

Someone (anonymous strikes again! &#x1f600;) blessed us with a bunch of Lego sets several days ago, and the most striking thing happened.  I had sat down with the kiddos to set each of them up with a Lego set, and they got to work.  Her nausea has been gradually fading away, so she asked if I could make some mac and cheese.  I stepped out of the room and into the kitchen (still within eyesight), and had to do a double take.  She didn't come with me - she continued to play Legos with Micah.  They talked, laughed, built, and overall just continued to have a grand old time with each other. 

She wasn't scared for me to be 10 feet away from her for the first time in months. 

I felt guilty for the times I had gotten frustrated about her being glued to my hip because it makes a ton of sense, right?  If I knew that a seizure was coming sometime in the next 5 minutes, I'd NEVER want to be alone without a parent.  How terrifying!  

But more than anything, I felt thankful.  

I'm now grappling with my last post about outcomes.  I catch myself saying things like, "God is so good - he took the nausea away", or "God is so good - the surgery seems to have worked and healed her".  Well... yes.  And no.  I'm realizing that YES God is so good, but NO, not due to the outcome of my situation.  God is good regardless of the "good" or "bad" in my life.  So... if I'm being intentional about my word choice here, what is accurate?  

I am thankful.  I'm so incredibly thankful.   Thankful that Lyla is gaining her sassy little personality back, that her facial expressions are back, her wit is coming back, processing speed is gradually getting faster, etc. 

**I'm selfishly thankful that God's choice of path with her situation was the one I was hoping for, but I think that might be ok if my faith isn't hinging on that outcome.  &#x1f642;**
