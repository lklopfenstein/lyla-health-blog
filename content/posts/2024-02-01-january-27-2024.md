The radiologists aren’t all on the same page. One thinks the images show a malformation and the other thinks it shows a tumor or could be autoimmune epilepsy due to cancer somewhere in the body. They got a third opinion from another radiologist who thinks any of these three things could be a possibility. They did an ultrasound of her belly looking for tumors, which they found none. Thank you Jesus!

It is still being debated whether the brain image is a malformation or a tumor. If it is a tumor it is likely benign as they haven’t seen growth between the first MRI and now. I discussed with them Aunt Ruth’s history, especially since the area in the brain is the same area and side. They all agreed that this does not look like a glioblastoma as that type of tumor has a very specific look which is much different than what they are seeing. The board is planning on meeting again on Monday to discuss her case further. If it is decided that they think it is a tumor then the resection surgery will be soon (without the sEEG), as time will be of the essence.  Otherwise, the initial plan of sEEG and then surgery 7 weeks later would remain the plan.

Fun fact: the room next to us at the hospital has seen 6 different patients in the time that we've been here.
