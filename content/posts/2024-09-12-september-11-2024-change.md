Howdy folks!  As I type, I'm sitting here with a completely tuckered out Lyla, watching my Buckeyes win the 2016 semifinal (Alabama) and final (Oregon) again because sometimes you just need to relive a game or two, ya know?  Like, life is full of so much uncertainty that sometimes a guaranteed win is what I'm craving.  

**Lyla Changes:
**We started 3rd grade!  As worried as we were about it being super difficult (given her post-surgery deficits) and possibly triggering seizures with the extra surge in brain activity, I'd say it's mostly been great.  The school worked with us incredibly well to provide the resources we needed including a paraprofessional to help throughout each day.  Lyla has been making new friends (and continuing some old friendships!), and that has been a major answer to prayer - that socially, this stuff wouldn't affect the ability to make friends and do things with friends.  So far, so good.

School is incredibly challenging when wrote memorization is out the window.  No flash cards for math facts, reading, vocab, and spelling make it all super challenging.  That said, she's putting in tons of effort, and it's paying off.  So far, despite the struggle... straight A's!

Seizure wise, we're still sitting at about 1 focal every 3-4 days, and about 3-5 auras per day.  However, the auras are now mostly super short - around 1 second long.  No focals yet at school, so fingers crossed there for now.  The focals are presenting a little differently, and honestly pretty scary.  It's weird... the focals seem to be spaced out JUST enough that we get perhaps a little overly confident.  Then, BOOM.  Focal.  Definitely keeping us on our toes.  She had a major one tonight and Micah helped me administer the rescue med.  Again... the maturity of this kid.  He has his moments of immaturity for sure (I mean, c'mon... he's 11), but what a great brother to assume a role of a caretaker of sorts.  I don't wish that on anyone, but he's for sure a better kid for it.

She got a sweet new haircut.  She's been BEGGING us to let her shave the side of her head so people will ask about her scar.  She simply wants to tell others about Jesus and how He helped her through the hardest time of her life so far.  She continues to impress me with how spiritually mature she is at age 8!

**House Changes:**
Not much to update here - still down to the studs, and quotes/estimates are happening behind the scenes.  However, about a week or so ago, we went over there as a family to draw pictures and write scripture, song lyrics, and quotes all over the house - on the studs, the floor, whatever.  It was an absolute blast, and hopefully a core memory for these kiddos.  I know it was for me! 

**Kayla Changes:**
It's her turn to be on a missions trip!  She left for Uganda last Thursday, and we're awaiting her much anticipated return this Saturday.  We've heard a few stories already, but can't wait to hear EVERYTHING.  More info to come here.

**Lee Changes**:
I'm going to go just a little bit light on the details with the first change just because I don't want to make anyone feel bad, but here's the gist: 

1. I've been paying for COBRA coverage and it's been insanely expensive, but when the alternative is over a half million dollars in healthcare costs... you suck it up and do what's best for the fam.
2. COBRA was about to run out at the end of 2024
3. We were gifted a sum of money to help pay for COBRA (a quote from that fateful late 2023 afternoon - "This won't get you the whole year, but it should get you through August or so")
4. I was told about an opening as a Senior OnBase Developer at Princeton University in August.  I had 5 interviews in 6 business days.  I've never seen a hiring process move so quickly!
5. Our gifted money ran out, and I am gearing up to start my new (fully remote) job at Princeton on Sept. 16th, which is the very day my new benefits kick in.  

Our prayer about healthcare is now answered, but I hesitate saying it like that.  I want to recognize here that God has been around a bit longer than insurance.  He could've just as easily provided what we needed without insurance and without a new job.  He also could've decided that maybe bankruptcy would've been the best way to prepare us for our next "Battle", whatever that may be - He simply chose to answer it this way.  Regardless of the position it puts ME in, God's plan is still good!

On a more fun note... I lost my acoustic guitar to the fire (nope... not the fun part).  It was sitting on a guitar stand in the living room out in the open and took some seriously heavy smoke damage.  I received a chunk of money from insurance to replace it, and I've just kind of been sitting on it.  I wanted to be open to the fact that maybe God doesn't want me to have one during this season.  To reference a prior post, perhaps he doesn't want me HIDING behind my guitar?  Anyway, we went to Guitar Center in Columbus around my birthday back in July.  I played a few from a specific brand, decided THAT was the brand, then honed in on a great fit of a guitar for me, sound-wise.  We didn't buy it.  Fast forward to last Thursday night - I hosted my small group at our house, and one of my friends mentioned, "Hey, aren't you looking for a $X- dollar guitar??  Jonathan G. (Our mutual friend that I probably hadn't seen in maybe 4 years?) is selling his for the exact same amount."  I skeptically looked at the post and.... it's THAT brand, but a model or two up.  Found out he's selling because it didn't really get played, and he wanted it to go to someone he knew.  I went to go see him and hear/play the guitar, and honestly, the conversation and time together reconnecting was sweeter than the tone of that amazing little guitar.  His battle with Parkinson's had made playing the acoustic pretty challenging/frustrating.  I totally get why he'd like his guitar to be in good hands... I would too.  I bought it.  

Jonathan... I promise I'll take good care of it, and use it as a tool to further God's kingdom.  God intersected our stories in such a unique way, and he used a simple tool - a guitar - to make our paths cross again.  Love ya man!

And to that end... God has chosen to put that tool in my tool belt again after a brief hiatus.  It's now on me to prayerfully make sure that tool is used how He wants.

**Some relevant scripture that doesn't change (Nor does God):**

- Malachi 3:6 - "I am the Lord. I do not change..."

- Hebrews 13:8 - "Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, today, and forever."

- Psalm 102:27 - "But You never change.  And Your life will never end."

And finally... a relevant prayer/liturgy taken from "Every Moment Holy, Vol. II":

**A Liturgy for Seasons of Uncertainty:**
In the midst of whatever follows, O Lord, let me meet your mercies anew, and anew, and anew.  In the midst of my dismay, fix my eyes again and again upon your eternal promises.

How this ends - that is up to You.  If the next news is favorable, I will praise You for the ongoing gift of life.  If tomorrow's tidings are worse, still will I proclaim Your goodness, my heart anchored ever more firmly in the eternal joys You have set before me. 

And when, whether days or decades from now, You finally bid me rise and follow You across the last valley, I will rejoice in Your faithfulness even there:  Especially there - praying _Thy will be done_, and trusting by faith that it _will_ be done.  That it _is_ being done. Even now.  Even in this disquiet.  

I am utterly Yours, O Christ.  In the midst of this uncertainty, I abandon myself again to You, the author and the object of all my truest hopes.

Amen.

Love you all.  My prayer is again that this brings a bit of hope and joy to your lives.  Have a wonderful evening, y'all... 

And to Kayla, Micah, and Lyla, I love you all with my entire life.  Let's continue to point each other to God first - the one that never changes.
