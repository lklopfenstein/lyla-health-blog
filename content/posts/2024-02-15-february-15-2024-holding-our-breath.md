**Song on repeat is Lyla's favorite**:
["Firm Foundation"](https://open.spotify.com/track/4iaas0o0bFzalTLwhQTuBH?si=bc1a3c6510064259) - Cody Carnes. (Or as she calls it, "Firm FoundationS" :) 

It's happening.  Brain surgery as I type this out.  Maybe it's weird that I'm typing out a post in this moment?  Honestly, I simply don't know what to do with my hands right now.  Stomach has been upset and in knots for quite a while.

Super emotional.  
A constant state of tense. 
Crinkled forehead. 
Collectively holding our breath.  

With grim/fearful thoughts of worst-case scenario creeping into the backs of our heads, we can't help but think... "God sent his only child to die for me?"  It really puts a new perspective on the foundation of my/our faith that we've believed for so long.  A new perspective on love. 

Sorry everyone if this shows just how little faith I have, but I can't imagine a love that would willingly give up my daughter for the sake of someone else, let alone the world.  That's crazy love.  I guess I don't measure up in that regard, but maybe that's part of what makes Him God (and me, definitely NOT God).  

I feel Lyla has also learned a good bit about love and posture over the past few months.  One particular example was during worship at church several weeks back.  We noticed she had gotten on her knees while singing songs to God with her arms up.  I don't believe she's seen anyone do that before.  Afterward, we had asked her, "What was going through your mind when you did that?"  Her reply was simple, yet profound.  

**"Well, I guess it just felt right to make myself smaller and God bigger."**

Please, in honor of what Lyla is going through, my prayer for anyone reading this today is that we all take _some_ step of faith that makes ourselves smaller and God bigger. 

My commitment will be this: Today, I will be less selfish and show the love of God through ACTION to someone today - namely Kayla, Micah, and Lyla.  

What's yours?
