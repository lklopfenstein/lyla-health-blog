**Update**:

Lyla has now had 3 focal seizures over the past week, and after calling up to Cleveland Clinic for guidance on next steps, we were directed toward coming back up for a video EEG to see what's going on.  Our wonderful team of doctors gave us the go-ahead to wait until after church on Sunday to head north, so that's what we did - well, half of us anyway.  Unfortunately, we felt it was necessary to divide and conquer, so I'm holding down the fort at home with Micah (And special thanks to our awesome friends for helping care for Micah while I work!!) while Kayla takes the lead up in Cleveland with Lyla.  So far... zero focals to speak of.  That's good... and bad.  Good that she's not seizing, but now we have essentially a blank EEG so far.  We'd love to see where they are originating in the brain and have SOME data and details, so yeah... we sort of want a seizure or two at this point - but please... emphasis on the sort of!  

Our amazing team in Cleveland is starting to ween her off a couple meds, which is awesome due to some of the side effects we've been seeing.  We know this could "help" with finding a seizure or two as well, but I think Kayla and I both worry that when we open up that door, we might actually end up opening the floodgates and will struggle to shut that door again.  To be clear, we have NOT been told that that's a possibility - just something I'm able to worry about, so I am (Don't worry, I understand I sound ridiculous. ha). 

**Spiritual thoughts**:

There's a piece of this story that I don't think I've mentioned before.  Lyla struggled with leaving the hospital after surgery.  It's hard to know what exactly was driving that - was it her missing her new best friends that she met and spent nearly a month straight with? (Anthony, Amaya, Andrew, Jen, Dr. Lachhwani, Dr. Pestana-Knight, Morgan, oh no who am I missing???  There's just too many lovely people up there.  I guess that's a wonderful problem to have!), was it the fact that she had gotten comfortable with living in the hospital?  Did she fear leaving?   Was the hospital her safe retreat?  Perhaps it's such a complex and complicated set of feelings that it's too hard put on an 8-year old to describe (days after brain surgery, no less), or maybe it's simply a mixture of it all.  Nonetheless... she had a lot of sad feelings leaving the hospital.  

Mixed with that, she's often said "I just don't feel like myself".  Wow.  What a phrase to unpack.  What is "herself"?  Prior to her first seizure?  During the phase of many seizures/before surgery?  Post-surgery?  Through several conversations with her, I've been able to gather that she doesn't like or want the seizures, but feels it's a part of who she is.  She's learning to have seizures remain part of her story as scars (and some quite literal!).  It's been beautiful to watch - she wants to keep the side of her head shaved.  She told me the other day that wants to get a TATTOO (yo... not ready for my 8-year old girlie to say that yet) of her question mark shaped stitches/scar.   Another thing I wasn't ready for?  Her reasoning why:

**"I just think that I'd like to have something about me or on me that people might look at and ask me about.  If they do, I want to tell them how God helped me through it, and maybe it can help them with what they're going through."**

Mannnn stop it.  I'm not crying, you're crying.  It's just been raining on my face, that's all.  Just so proud of her and her steadfast reliance upon God in the midst of everything.  All of this stuff, it's a mystery.  Yet God is so clearly riddled through all of it.... and she's leaning into it.
