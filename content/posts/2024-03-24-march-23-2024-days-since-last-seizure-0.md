Days since last seizure: 0

It happened today.  It's hard to describe how it felt exactly as all four of us were right there together as it happened.  I'll try it though: Devastation.  A realization that this nightmare probably isn't over in the way that we thought.  Literally collapsing to my knees in fear.  Instant tears.  The thought of "What options do we have AFTER brain surgery??  Like... what else IS there, even??"

Sweet Lyla was very talkative, energetic, joyful, and even apologetic to us afterward.  Yes, apologetic.  She was sad for us and sorry that it scared us.  She told us many times not to worry about it, and even prayed over the three of us.  I can't believe I'm even typing that out - the maturity (spiritual and emotional) of this 8-year-old girl is unprecedented.  Later on in the car, a song called "Walk with Me" by GoldFord (not a worship song) came on and she sang it to us (totally new perspective on this tune - lyrics below).

Processing this tonight has been kinda all over the place.  I'm thankful that Kay and I remain incredibly unified - broken for sure, but unified.  I go back to the post about outcomes - how we don't need the outcomes we want in order to know that God is good, kind, love, etc. and well... here's an opportunity.  We had a new outcome tonight that sucked royally.  God is still good and I have confidence that He will continue to bring beauty out of all of this.  **Maybe there's a miracle yet to happen in someone's life** - could be Lyla, a friend, a family member, a complete stranger even!  I think it's simply on me to trust that God will continue to use her story for His glory and honor.
