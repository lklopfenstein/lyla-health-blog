Some tough ones the past few days.  :( 

During our most recent 2 week stay at the Cleveland Clinic, they put her on Dilantin and it brought the seizure count down to nothing for 3 and a half days.  However, as a potent med, they need to keep checking the levels of Dilantin in her blood (I think she's had 4 blood draws now over the past few weeks).  Unfortunately, the Dilantin levels keep coming back too high, so they reduce the dosage and then... number of bigger seizures per day rises.  With the most recent change, we went from 12 larger seizures per day to 26 (in addition to all the little ones!)  We've had to give her rescue med a handful of times now over the past few days because she's RIGHT at the threshold for giving it (3 larger seizures in an hour).  We've also been living at the threshold for taking her to the E.R. (needing the rescue med a second time after four hours have passed). 

Needless to say... it's been pretty stressful living in this constant state of "E.R??  Not yet... NOW??  Maybe!  YES. NO!! Not yet!  Definitely NOW - NO WAIT.  She's good now - she's sleeping.  Time to go to bed." 

Ok, so time to take a deep breath.  What does the Bible have to say?  I opened up the book of James tonight and read this in chapter 1:

"My brothers, you will have many kinds of troubles.  But when these things happen, you should be very happy.  You know that these things are testing your faith.  And this will give you patience.  Let your patience show itself perfectly in what you do.  Then you will be perfect and complete.  You will have everything you need." 

How fitting hearing about patience (and I'm remembering from a prior post - not the ability to wait, but how we act while waiting) when life has me/us so intensely on edge.  Patience... the surgery is scheduled.  It's going to happen.  

Quick reminders to myself:

- Have faith that God is in control.  
- Take heart in knowing that Lyla doesn't need a different story.  EDIT: check out this relevant song - [Story - Kings Kaliedescope](https://open.spotify.com/track/1MeQJmRY0bhWHBifD2dL7I?si=cO-hhowoTBScvgbFy-pV8Q)
- She can have (and is having!) a wonderful impact on those around her with this one right here.  
- God doesn't promise an easy life.  In fact, on the contrary... James 1 tells us "... you WILL have many kinds troubles... ", not might, maybe, if, etc. 
- I will snap at others when I remain on edge... time to calm down and surrender control so Kayla and I can remain a unified front.  How unfortunately easy it is to turn against the ones we love the most :(  Both Kayla and Micah are both deserving of an apology and an ask for forgiveness and re-centering in love and prayer tonight!
- God has used family, friends, distant relatives, anonymous donors, and strangers alike to provide literally EVERYTHING we need and more (Electric toothbrush, starlight, pillowcases - SERIOUSLY ANONYMOUS, WHO ARE YOU AND HOW THE HECK DID YOU KNOW??? :D, stuffies, blankies, a surprise visit from Uncle Mark that perked her up like crazy, a cross necklace, house cleaning, a killer home-cooked meal, and so much more just in the past couple of days. 

On that last point, It's been so humbling.  We cry at the littlest thing now.  I think that's us continuing to learn about the two-sided coin that is love.
