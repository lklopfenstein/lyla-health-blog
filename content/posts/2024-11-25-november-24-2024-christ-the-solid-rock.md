**Wow. I am simply amazed at the amount of people willing to give a few minutes of their day to beg and plead with God on our daughter's behalf. We're truly humbled and grateful for every prayer offered. It's hard to imagine why even complete strangers would be so kind to us. Whatever the reason, though... thank you. It means the world to us.**

**There was a point in time in West Virginia where Kay and I had hiked a beautiful fall-colored rail trail to a series of waterfalls. We had stopped to pray/sob/yell/plead with God over all the Lyla stuff, and when we were done... received that text about Psalm 40:1-3, but also had a song in our hearts. We had been sitting on leaves/grass, but decided to stand hand in hand, step forward to the rocky edge of the path overlooking the main waterfall, and just once, simply sing these words from an old hymn:

"On Christ the solid rock I stand, all other ground is sinking sand.
 All other ground is sinking sand."

And that was it. Just a few seconds of authentic worship with not a soul around us. It was a beautiful moment for us. I'd also like to share the rest of that song with anyone who will listen because of the power of its truth. It was encouraging for us, so I'm hopeful it can encourage someone else too. Here goes...**

**"My hope is built on nothing less than Jesus' blood and righteousness
I dare not trust the sweetest frame but wholly lean on Jesus' name
On Christ the solid rock I stand, all other ground is sinking sand"

Thank God I'm not in control. I'm broken, self-centered, prideful, you name it. If I were in control of the world, I'd most certainly ruin it. If God is in control, I can rest knowing the outcome is already perfect - exactly how it should be to bring God the most glory (and to be clear, NOT to bring Lee or the Klopfenstein's the most glory). So that's where our hope will be, even when that's difficult to sing due to uncertainty of our family's future. All other ground is sinking sand.

"When darkness veils His lovely face, I'll rest on His unchanging grace
In every high and stormy gale, My anchor holds within the veil
On Christ the solid rock I stand, all other ground is sinking sand"**

**It kinda terrifies me that God will do whatever He wants/needs to bring Him the most glory. I'm scared of where that'll leave my daughter. Scared of where that'll leave us as a family.**

**The doctors pulled Lyla off a lot of her meds this week in order to trigger seizures during testing (I should say a DECENT amount of testing - SPECT, MEG, Ictal SPECT, VEEG, etc.). At first, it was surprisingly light, even having a ZERO day (day with no seizures/auras/belly feelings/etc), and it's been months since the last one. Then... she started having some intense focal seizures on Thursday, and on Friday, her first generalized seizures since June 2023. They've since given the meds back, and she's trending in the right direction again, albeit with a lot of intense emotions due to the onboarding of meds all at once. It's easy to look back on the last week and see the darkness of it, but you know what? There was some beauty to be had as well. At the beginning of the week, when she first started coming off the meds, "old" Lyla started emerging. Very expressive facially, silly, sweet, kind. It was such a breath of fresh air to see her like that. That's my girl!! The darkness of the latter part of the week clearly tries to hide God's lovely glimpse of our Lyla that we received. I'm choosing to rest in His unchanging grace, because that glimpse was so beautiful. All other ground is sinking sand.**

**"His oath, His covenant, His blood support me in overwhelming flood
When all around my soul gives way, He then is all my hope and stay.
On Christ the solid rock I stand, all other ground is sinking sand"

Internally, this is all a lot for us to handle. I worry about Lyla. I worry if I'm establishing a good enough balance between the kids - giving Lyla the attention she needs while still being able to give Micah the attention he needs even if his needs aren't the same. I worry about the future - heck, I'm dealing with some severe anxiety these days. Some days are just hard to take the next step. I'm learning that God shows himself in both good and bad, but I somehow find it so surprising that God's love and mercy are so evident in the bad. It's as if God is in the business of providing peace/hope/mercy/grace to the broken or something. So that's where I'll attempt to put my hope. All other ground is sinking sand.**

**"When He shall come with trumpet sound, Oh, may I then in Him be found
Dressed in His righteousness alone, faultless to stand before the throne
On Christ the solid rock I stand, all other ground is sinking sand...

And when that day finally comes when our suffering is over (either healing or a metaphor for heaven), what a joyous day that will be for our family. Maybe at that time, this song will be easy to sing. For now, it'll be tear-filled. It'll be choking through the words. It'll be a shaky voice at the very least. Maybe a whisper. But I can't keep going without staking my claim in this. I think sometimes worship is a declaration of standing firm with God when life is hardest and the song can hardly come out... and you push through and try to sing it anyway.**

**Because...**

**"...all other ground is sinking sand"**
