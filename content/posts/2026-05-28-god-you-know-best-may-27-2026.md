**Prayer to Start:**
God, make Your name great above all else, even if that comes at the expense of my intelligence, dignity, pride, whatever... If you can use these words to bring someone closer to You, then so be it - I pray that furthering Your kingdom always matters more to me than my own comfort.  God, take away my own words and use Your own.  Help me get out of the way and simply use this humble little site to put Your love and mercy on full display.

**Lyla update:**
Baltimore was a truly great experience.  Dr. Hartman (Rasmussen's) and Dr. Chen (Neuroimmunology) worked together in concert so beautifully and gave value to every piece of the puzzle.  It was more of a fact-finding mission for them than a providing of answers to us.  He has a phone conversation with Dr. Pestana on Friday, but honestly... it's probably just a formality.  

The month of May has not been kind to Lyla.  She has seen an explosion of generalized seizures lasting anywhere between 2 and 5 minutes where we are forced to lay her down on her right side to open up a path for air to breathe.  We see drooling, eyes and head rolling back to the right side, facial twitching, limbs stiffening/curling, but then... scarier stuff is starting to enter the picture more often - she'll stop breathing altogether and face turns a blue-ish gray.  On May 19th, she saw 5 generalized seizures, we rescued twice (That's the daily limit, by the way), and went to the ER.  We ended up in Cleveland for an unexpected stay from May 19th - May 22nd to monitor things in the EMU.  She did well there with some substantial med increases and probably due to the fact that she stayed in bed and barely moved.  During this stay, Dr. Pestana showed us the results of her latest MEG scan with overlays showing her MEG scans from before her 1st surgery and before her 2nd surgery.  The one from late April showed pretty clear spreading of the disease.  That paired with nearly all of her seizures now containing a right arm motor component, and we've now got a pretty clear direction of where this is headed.

We went home and proceeded to have 7 more generalized seizures over the holiday weekend.  We came back to the Cleveland Clinic EMU on Monday, May 25th and just came back home this evening, May 27th.  The surgical board met on Tuesday while we were there to discuss Lyla's case.  The same board that was split back in the fall was no longer split this time.  They unanimously agreed that

1) This is indeed Rasmussen's Encephalitis
2) She must have the surgery we've been dreading - a Hemispherectomy
3) She needs it soon - like... in a few weeks (Will just depend on Dr. Bingaman's schedule/availability)

Devastated.

Absolutely devastated.  

They gave us a solid rescue plan and a med change that seems to be doing ok so far, but it's just a band-aid.  We know that even if we see a zero day (or 2, or 3...), it doesn't change the fact that she needs this surgery to stop it from spreading to the right hemisphere.  

We've been crying a ton because we know what it means for her long-term, physically and cognitively.  Post-surgery this time will entail at least a full month of in-patient PT/OT/Speech, but even with that in place... this will take from her a lot of the things she loves to do.  

**Prayer:**
God - I pray that Lyla is able to do more for Your kingdom with one good half of a brain than most people can do with two good halves.  I pray for a double portion of Your Spirit for her. 

**Some relevant scripture**:
Jeremiah, as a prophet, was asked by the people to pray that "the Lord your God will tell us where we should go and what we should do" in Jeremiah 42:3, and in verse 6, go on to say " Whether it is favorable or unfavorable, we will obey the Lord our God, to whom we are sending you, so that it will go well with us, for we will obey the Lord our God."

Jeremiah heard from the Lord ten days later telling him in verse 10, "If you stay in this land, I will build you up and not tear you down", continuing in verse 13, "However, if you say, 'We will not stay in this land,' and so disobey the Lord your God, and if you say, 'No, we will go and live in Egypt, where we will not see war or... then the sword you fear will overtake you there."

In Jeremiah 43:2, the people call Jeremiah a liar and directly disobey God's word to them.  Essentially revealing that the people were just wanting God to confirm to them that His plan was what they desired all along.  God was the afterthought to their plan.

In our situation, God has been faithful all along, no matter how much pain has been brought to this household.  This surgery has been a real possibility from the very beginning - just the one path that we haven't wanted anything to do with.  So do we reject it?  Or do we finally submit this last piece we've been holding on to over to God and say - God, You know best.  If this is how You'll make Your name great, then so be it - provide her the ability to wildly further Your kingdom and show Your great love and mercy with only a right hemisphere.  If anything wavers, let it never be her love and desire for You.  Let her continue to be the loudest worshiper on Sunday mornings at church, in the car, and at home with her headphones on... 

... and if she's unable to raise her own right hand in worship, give me the boldness to stand in the gap and raise my own.
