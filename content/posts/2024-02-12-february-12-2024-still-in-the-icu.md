Since arriving in the Pediatric ICU, we've upped her daily meds and put her on a drip of a new med that is pretty potent and requires constant monitoring of her respirations and possible breathing tube.  We've had to increase that dosage 5 times and also had a bolus dose, but her respirations have remained great.  So to this point, we still haven't had a need to intubate.  However, with the possibility of intubation, she hasn't been allowed to eat or drink.  Go figure... she actually WANTS to eat and drink now.  

With the latest increase in meds, the seizures have started to improve in count and intensity, so that's super positive.  We'll stay in the ICU and monitor while keeping the dosage where it's at.  The goal is to eventually ween off of it and step down to a normal Peds room.  We're also hoping to hear from Dr. Bingaman (Her neurosurgeon) and Dr. Pestana-Knight (Her doctor) today to figure out what the plan is for surgery - possible to move up?  If not, what does the "bridge" look like to get from here to Feb. 23rd?  

UPDATE: At rounds today, they said that they were able to get in touch with Dr. Bingaman and ask if surgery can be moved up.  Apparently, he gave a one-word response: "Sure".  lol.  They're now working on scheduling,  then we'll be able to see in clearer detail what a bridge plan looks like.  Also, she can now start to have small sips of water and apple juice.

Yesterday was emotional as it was the day she was supposed to be baptized at church.  Trying to stay positive through it - we understand that God has a plan here that's much bigger picture than what we're thinking. :)  Intensely looking forward to when it DOES happen! 

**Song in our heads today** - Highlands (Song of Ascent):
[Highlands - Song of Ascent](https://open.spotify.com/track/78bUGqKRoXN8kJY1AeWhzt?si=274949b28e494a1b)

"I will praise You on the mountain, I will praise You when the mountain's in my way
You're the summit where my feet are, so I will praise You in the valleys all the same
No less God within the shadows, no less faithful when the night leads me astray
You're the heaven where my heart is, in the highlands and the heartache all the same"

This feels appropriate today.  Over the past 24-48 hours, we've experienced some "mountains in our way", and although it may seem opposite from most, I feel it's easier and more natural to turn to God when there's nowhere else to turn.  Complete dependence on God.  But what about when things start getting better?  What about when nothing's wrong?  I know the right answer here, but... When I'm honest, to what degree do I live with a dependence on God?  Or maybe a cringe-ier question is, How much do I actually NEED God when there aren't issues that I'm/We're dealing with? 

Yikes.  Perhaps I need to use these lyrics as my prayer as we head toward this path of restoration for Lyla.  A prayer to remain spiritually broken and dependent upon God, yet aiming for growth/restoration/healing.
