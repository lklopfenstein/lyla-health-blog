I gotta admit... time is getting away from me.  We're long overdue for an update, so let's get after it!

**The House:**
It's finished! We moved back in on June 14th, and we're settling in.  Because of the fire, we had a great reminder of the possessions we have that truly matter (none of them), a reality check of everything we need (God), a redefinition of the word "Home" (simply a place where our family is together),  an understanding of just how little I control (and a new understanding of how little I really WANT to control!), and a repeating bit of scripture in our hearts ('The Lord’s love never ends. His mercies never stop. They are new every morning. Lord, your loyalty is great. I say to myself, “The Lord is what I have left. So I have hope.”' - Lamentations 3:22-24).

God... thank you for the blessing of a new house, but even more so... thank you for the lessons learned.  Thank you for stripping us down to nothing and building us back up again.  Thank you for the metaphor of redemption - showing us through our house (trivial) what you are able to do with people (meaningful) - you make them new.  Let us use our home to welcome in and love others.  We pray that you help us get out of the way so you can work.  You are compelling enough to follow all by Yourself. Use our house however you desire.  Amen.

**A Youth Conference and Guatemala round 2:**
Just days after move in, Kayla had the opportunity to spend a week in Bowling Green with our church youth and lead a small group of girls.  It was tough timing, but God used her in some really cool ways.  I'm so proud of her and her "Yes" to that call in her life.  Keep crushing it, KK!

A day or so after she came back to town, I left on a trip of my own.  Again... tough timing, but wonderful experience. I am so excited to share that not only did I get a second opportunity to head back to Guatemala, but thanks to a generous gift from some friends... was able to take Micah with me.  He was able to meet some of the wonderful people from the Carepoint that I had met last year - Jomara, Carlos, Xiomara, Aldair, Chepe, Charlie, Jose, Yessi, Dairin, Handy, Betsy, and MANY MANY others.  He was able to play soccer several times with the kiddos and loved it.  

I felt incredibly inadequate to guide Micah through such an experience that is even challenging for an adult to process, but my prayer was simply this: that God would tug on his heart and instill a desire to return.  

He came back to Ohio with lots of cool stories, life experiences, and perhaps even a little global perspective on life, but my favorite win... wanting to return.  Not to return in order to go places or do specific things, but rather... return so he can see Dairin again and talk to her.  To cook with Julio again.  To play soccer yes, but more so to play with his new friends.  Way to go, bud.  Way to value relationships. :) 

**Lyla update:**
It's been a bit of a mixed bag, but here's the gist: 
- IVIG ended.  She had her 6th infusion and we stopped due to it being ineffective
- Seizure count has been mostly the same (maybe 3-5 focals per week and about 3-5 auras per day), but we've been seemingly using a lot more of the rescue meds.  Not sure if that's indicative of anything, just something we're noticing. 
- After a year of Steroids, she's finally titrating down off of them! :)
- Attitude has now gotten to a point where I feel like I should address it.  Partly due to coming off the steroids, partly because of a new med, and partly due to the part of her brain that was removed... she's having some major anger and rage meltdowns.  She's saying and doing things that are completely unlike her.  It's hard to know what to do as parents because what do we do?  Discipline her for not having the right fear or anger response when she doesn't have an Amygdala?  We're doing our absolute best we can, but would certainly appreciate a showering of prayers in this area!!
- Our team of doctors are directing us down the path of a second brain surgery.  It's hard to know what to hope/pray for, but our next step is to go in for an SEEG - an invasive EEG requiring surgery to insert nodes into the brain to get a better picture (with some depth) of where the seizures are stemming from.  This will give us information we haven't had to this point, so I guess we'll just wait and see what we find.  This will start on August 15th, and could last anywhere between 10 days and a month.  

I continue to pray daily every 6 hours for Lyla.  12:00 noon remains a time for me to plead with God for complete and restorative healing for her, while I've transitioned 6am, 6pm, and midnight to a prayer telling God that I trust Him with the journey and the outcome, and pray for grace to trust Him more.  I welcome anyone willing to join the same or similar cadence on behalf of Lyla. :)

**Song on Repeat:**
No specific song this time around that is on repeat... I've just been throwing [Lyla's playlist](https://open.spotify.com/playlist/5oi9e2cT5JHBPsXmS4OI5l?si=a8GNLsgfTRen5Mapc2lrew) as a whole on repeat.

**Final Prayer:**
God, I recognize our burdens are great when compared to some folks, and minuscule when compared to others.  Whatever our burdens, I thank You for them.  So far they have grown our faith and unified our family under You. Our story makes Your work and Your love obvious despite our own shortcomings. I pray that as our own story develops and progresses that we can continue to see Your beauty even in what we might see as the most extreme crises.  We love You and pray for more opportunities to share Your love with others while we get out of the way and let you go to work.  Amen.
