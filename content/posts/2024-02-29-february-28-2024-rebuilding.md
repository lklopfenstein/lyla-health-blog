"Dad... I'm getting that belly feeling again."  

These are the dreaded words that we would hear from Lyla whenever she experiences a sensation in her stomach that she describes as going down a steep rollercoaster hill - the doctors call this an "aura" and it forewarns us that a focal seizure is likely going to happen within about 20-30 seconds.  Sometimes it ended after 30 seconds and never grew to a focal, sometimes it did. 

I don't know if it's some form of PTSD or what, but I swear I've "heard" her say that phrase probably a billion times over the past two weeks (when she hasn't) or had _some_ unique body movement that looked like the beginnings of focal seizure, etc. There's been all these phantom things that I keep "hearing" or "seeing", but it's gradually getting less and I'm super thankful for that. :) 

Now... I don't want to make too big a deal over this because the doctors told us it might happen, but this week she actually DID start having a handful of "Belly feelings" that have NOT turned into focal seizures.  I don't know mentally what to do with that exactly, but I'm still saying that she's been seizure-free since her surgery as she hasn't had a single focal seizure.  

We're rolling with it.  Outside of a handful of "belly feelings", she's been great.  Stitches came out today, so I'm hoping that a before and after picture isn't gross or inappropriate to put on here... It's a joyous step for us and kind of like, "Hey look at this!! *points to scar* Notice anything different???  *Beaming*". So I truly hope you all don't mind!  

The song below felt relevant to me personally as I have a history of blaming God for the things that have gone wrong in my life.  This whole thing with Lyla - it's been a pretty big one... **and I think I'm finally willing to admit that I was wrong.**  Wrong in years past about who God is, who He's been all along.  God's love, mercy, goodness, kindness... even in the midst of all the garbage... has been so incredibly present and beautiful.

**Prayer for tonight:** (From Every Moment Holy - A Liturgy for Beginning to Rebuild) 

"Perhaps it is a subtle shift, O Lord, but I find myself now able to remember my loss (pain) with a sadness that does not inevitably give way to the pain I have known for so much of this journey.  For the first time in such a long time, I catch myself making plans, gathering back the fragments of life shattered by grief, and beginning to consider how those pieces might fit together again.  At such a milestone, I pause to acknowledge your goodness displayed in the slow work of your mercies rebuilding my life.  It is no small thing to find that I am able to face once more the details and decisions of life that so recently overwhelmed me.  I know there is yet a costly road I must travel from here, O Christ, but I can glimpse from this new vantage new evidences of your faithfulness that escaped my comrehension in that dark valley: how you have been at work all along... 

 

... Things will not be the same as they were.  I know this.  Life has taken on a different shape - one scarred by loss (pain) and furrowed by sorrow, but it takes shape with affirmations of your tender grace re-emerging from the long chaos of grief.  Meaning and purpose will not be permanent casualties of my loss.  This is what I am now given eyes to see afresh.  You are yet my God, my Shepherd, my King...

...So shape me and use the balance of my days, Father, for your holy purposes.  Use me however you will.  If it please you, use even what has been my great pain.  Use it for the good comfort of others.  In this vocation, let me imitate my Lord.  Fold the heartaches of this tragedy into the unfolding story of your greater redemption, granting that I would soon sense the return - not only of increased purpose and meaning - but also of renewed joy.

I have endured this night, O Christ.  Now let the burning edge of dawn fling its light over the soft curve of the world, announcing the defeat of a darkness already in retreat before the resurrected day.

Amen."
