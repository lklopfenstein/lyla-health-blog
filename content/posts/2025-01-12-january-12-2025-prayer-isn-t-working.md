Let me stop you real quick after reading that title.  If you've read the title, please promise me you'll finish reading this post before calling me out as a heretic! :) 

I can't believe just how much has happened since the last post less than two weeks ago.  She went for her first IVIg infusion this past week (unfortunately, it was supposed to be her first day back at school) and it went incredibly well... for the first day.  

The first night, she was up half the night vomiting and having severe headaches.  The following morning, she was still vomiting like crazy (sorry for this mental picture, guys) and couldn't keep her morning meds down.  She was running a pretty good fever too, so we kept her home from school.  She had a fairly intense focal around noon, and then promptly went into a full blown generalized seizure an hour after that.  We gave the rescue med, but she wouldn't come out of the seizure so we called 911.  

**_Prayer_**_: "God, I love her with all my heart.  Don't You??  Surely, You could show Your great power and love through healing, right?
_**_Perceived outcome_**_: God doesn't love her and he doesn't love our family.  If He did, things wouldn't look the way they do.  
_**_What if God DID answer?_**_: As if God were saying, "You think you know what love is?".  He has grown my capacity for love.  When Lyla had her first instance of pain in her right arm (pain in her right side is a sign of Rasmussen's Encephalitis and a sign that a hemispherectomy might be looming in the future), it felt completely helpless.  While sobbing, I told Kayla, "I think love in this moment means giving that girlie an arm massage."  I realize that doesn't sound groundbreaking or anything, but internally it was something new._

(Back to the update) We ended up in the ER wondering if she was experiencing "Aseptic Meningitis" that they had warned us about during IVIg as possible, yet unlikely.  Turns out, that's what it was.  It resolves on its own in a handful of days, so it sounds worse than it is.  

Fast forward to last night, she started experiencing some intense pain in her back, chest, and legs (uncontrollable shaking/screaming).  It felt so helpless because nobody who could help or provide guidance was on call or available to talk.  

**_Prayer_**_: "God... seriously more stuff???  Why us???  Have mercy on us already!!"
_**_Perceived outcome_**_: If there's a God, He's simply tossing us about with no regard for us.  "God is love?!  PSHT.  Where's the love in this?"
_**_What if God DID answer?_**_: With each new tragedy comes some element of beauty - A song, a box, a verse, a prayer... something.  Maybe it was never about the amount or severity of "the stuff", but rather my ability to see His mercy and love through all of it.  God is an expert in putting the right people together in the right times and right places.  Hindsight being 20/20 here, it's easy to look back on the different things I've viewed as "tragedies" with a new set of eyes - "I'd have never met and developed a friendship with ___ if it weren't for being in the hospital.", "I'd never have had an opportunity to interact with the fire chief in the "heat of the moment" (YES DAD JOKE) had it not been for the fire", etc.  As for "Why us?", well... Why not us?  Maybe God has entrusted US with these burdens giving us an opportunity to give Him the glory through it.  Is that... dare I say... a blessing?_

We ultimately found a decent position for her to sleep, and she woke up this morning able to walk around just fine after a wonderful night's sleep.  Kay and I are looking at each other kinda wide-eyed and shrugging a bit as if to say, "What is this, now??"  Near the end of church, she started experiencing some heavy drowsiness and headache/backache again, so at this point we feel like we're still kind of in the middle of it.

As a side note - after her major seizure on Wednesday, her seizure count has been pretty low (Thursday - 0, Friday - 1 aura, Saturday - 1 aura, Sunday - 2 auras so far), but that's usually what happens after a big seizure so we're on high alert again probably starting today.  

We've talked with Neuroimmunology about all this hoping IVIg wasn't off the table as it's the only thing currently ON the table.  We feel better after talking with them because we know now there are things they can do to mitigate a lot of what she experienced.  Also, they let us know that the first infusion is usually where patients experience the worst side effects.  So all that considered... we are starting to feel a little better about moving forward with IVIg in hopes that the second infusion and beyond will go over much better.

**_Prayer_**_: "God, I trust You with Lyla.  I beg You to perform a miracle in her body!"
_**_Perceived outcome_**_: Nothing's happening.  Why is nothing happening?  I thought I told You that I trusted You!
_**_What if God DID answer?_**_: What if this is less about a miracle and more about me telling Him that first sentence over and over each day? He still has yet to promise me anything about a miracle for her little body, but God has certainly grown our trust in Him. _

So we continue with IVIg, and we continue to prep for Minnesota.  Is prayer working in the meantime?  100% yes, just... perhaps not in the way I had selfishly hoped for.  Prayer isn't transactional, and I don't want to pray selfish prayers any more.  

I feel the need to apologize to a lot of you.  We've had to admit to ourselves that we're back in "survival mode" and we simply don't have the capacity we had in prior months.  We've found ourselves needing to cancel things we've committed to, saying "No" to good things we'd normally commit to, etc.  It feels terrible, and we hope things will change in the near future.  If you're one of the many people that we've had to cancel on recently, please hear us - we love you.  We're simply trying to take care of our family and survive the day right now.  If you see us say "Yes" to someone else, it only means that that we had found a tiny bit of capacity somewhere, no more or less love. 

Much love to you all - have a most excellent Sunday!
