Wow.  This is the first post in... over 5 months.  Perhaps I should start with an apology.

If I'm being truly transparent, I think a lot of my radio silence is due to me dropping back into a bit of despair.  I'll admit, it's pretty wild living in this season because it seems as though we're living in two different worlds - one is where Lyla is having a great section of her day and all seems business as usual like life once was a few years ago - a life where we go for a walk in the neighborhood or play basketball in the driveway, or take some time to play a board game as a family.  The other would be when she has a 45-seizure-day like Friday where it's nonstop action or a day like Saturday where I saw one of the scariest seizures I've seen - She lost all color and no joke... stopped breathing until I gave her a rescue med.  

And then... 30 minutes later, we're right back into that first world.  We're at Micah's track meet cheering him on while she plays with some of her friends in the stands.  

What do we do with that?  I literally had just finished sobbing as I drove the 3 of us to the track meet and turn around and start having some normal conversations with some truly wonderful folks there.  I strongly desire life going back to normal, and I don't know if I even had a desire to talk about it with anyone in that moment, but I do think it's just a really wild thing to attempt to grapple with - this strange dichotomy of terror and normalcy living within 30 minutes of each other.  (For what it's worth, part of the reason for my tears in the car was listening to her sing a worship song - This kid, I tell ya...)

I'm realizing at least this bit: I have been so fixated on trying to control the situation and precautions for Lyla that when stability inevitably comes crashing down with a massive seizure or super busy seizure day, it starts to do something different to me.  I start to lose my sight for seeing the beauty of God in all of it.  I dive back into the woe-is-me style despair.  "God, are you REALLY on our side?", I'll say to myself (knowing the answer).  But it's almost coming from a place of wrestling against God.  Maybe I can do the reverse psychology thing here to strong-arm Him into healing Lyla, right?  

***Pausing here for eye roll***

C'mon, Lee.  You know better than this.  You have lived through some seriously beautiful moments of tragedy where God has remained so steadfast and present.  There's nothing to doubt here.  Maybe I need to flip the script and not focus so much of my attention on whether or not I perceive God to be on MY side.  

Wait... do I have a side?  Or was it God's side all along?  Perhaps I should focus my attention on staking my claim in this: **I will remain on God's side. ** God has MY back?  Or maybe... **I've got God's back**.  (Like He needs my support?  But hey, I think it's a good place for me to shift to mentally/spiritually)  God has a greater plan in play.  Too much evidence is present for me to believe or even begin to think otherwise.  _I'll attempt to live out my days with joy and have eyes for seeing the beauty of God in all circumstances._

**Quick Lyla update:**
We're still having lots of seizure activity - some of it being awfully scary.  Although it ebbs and flows day to day, we're probably averaging 20-30 seizures per day.  We drove out to Baltimore to Johns Hopkins today (May 11th) for an appointment on May 12th with the world's leader in Rasmussen's Encephalitis.  If it were only as easy as simply asking him the question, "Is this Rasmussen's or is it not?"!  I wish... but alas, the working theories today are 1. It's "Atypical Rasmussen's", showing some signs of it, but not others, or 2. There is still affected brain tissue that is in need of a third resection.  

In any case, it would seem as though we're heading toward a third brain surgery at the moment.  If it's Rasmussen's, it'd be either a Hemispherectomy (a surgery with severe lasting physical and cognitive effects like weak or paralyzed on right side, loss of peripheral vision, loss of language, etc.) or perhaps a trial run of a different type of infusion called Rituximab (similar to the IVIG infusions she did for 6 months).

Pretty nervous about next steps.  You'd think the infusion route would be the one I'd be hoping for, but no one we've talked to has great confidence in that truly having an effect - just another stone that's been unturned to this point.  That's all well and good, but the risk is pretty rough - it'll weaken her immune system to a point where we'd have to be very vigilant about keeping sickness away from her (wearing masks, being careful about where we go, who we're in contact with, etc.)

We want to do the best we possibly can for our girl, but I think sometimes for me anyway, I'm blurring a line between doing my part and control.  I get fixed on taking precautions for her - that's good... until that's what my faith is in.  I'll continue to give my best for the sake of my family, but I can do better with a complete dependence on God.  I heard this song earlier in the week and it made a lot of sense to me in the moment - here's hoping it means something to you too!
