Ok, so I know that it's been a bit.  My apologies if anyone has just been left wondering what's going on.  Quite honestly, it's been hard to keep up... in a good way!  My goodness, we have been making up for lost time in getting out of the house and doing things!  

We've had game nights at home and Masterminds (a favorite board game place in Ashland), a carnival at school, our return to kid's church, return to Awana, some outings to Cleveland and Columbus with some friends, and we're planning a small Spring Break getaway as well.  And to boot, we're planning a return to her classroom in Ontario after Spring Break.  

Everything is on track, and she just can't seem to get enough of all this new-found activity.  She has even expressed a new interest in [softball](https://www.facebook.com/164901175/videos/398757066254481) (so we're now signed up for that!) and has started back in on her gymnast bar downstairs.  She *might* have even performed a cartwheel, but if she did (and this is all hypothetical), her team of doctors can skip this part of the update :) 

So that's all great news, and she's doing some other things to aid in the recovery process as well - PT, OT, Speech, and Counseling are all part of the weekly routine.  We've also received word at the most recent follow-up appointment that we're going to start decreasing her seizure meds one at a time starting tomorrow. 

We also received results from pathology that what they removed from her brain was NOT cancerous, but rather a malformation that was likely there from birth.

**Some personal spiritual takeaways:**
We did a song at church a week or two ago called "More than Able".  This instance, I had the opportunity to sing it hand-in-hand with Kayla in such a shift of circumstances, but it reminded me of when we did this song back in December when things were really heating up with Lyla girl - we were very much in the thick of it.  Krista (my sister-in-law and worship leader at church) had me lead the congregation in that song, and it (or you could read, "I") was a complete and utter mess.  I could seemingly only choke through about every other lyric.  If you attend our church and didn't know why at the time... now you know! :)  For what it's worth... it was the most genuine and transparent and self-conscious I've been in my 20-some odd years leading worship.  

I still give Krista grief about that, but you know what?  **If it takes a few moments of emotional embarrassment in front of a congregation for even one individual at our church to experience just a moment of genuine worship of God, then so be it. ** I'm bought in now and it's worth it.

As a musician, I've had this weird relationship with worship music over the years (mostly hating it? Apologies to everyone for choosing rap, metal, and instrumental most of the time 😬 - please don't judge), but ever since starting a real relationship with God, there has been undeniable meaning and beauty throughout every time of worship.  Reading Scripture? Same. Reading liturgies?  Same.  

I'm listening and waiting now instead of my prior attitude of skepticism while crossing my arms and judging. 

On another note, I was scheduled to drum this week.  Because of a conflict, I had to quickly switch someone for next week, and guess what?  It's Easter, and I couldn't be more pumped.  Wait no... I can be more pumped -  I found out we're doing "Homecoming" for the first time at our church. 💪 God's timing is truly impeccable and uncanny. 

Ok, so here's some context, but I'll try to keep it to the cliff's notes as it's a much longer story.  I mentioned in a prior post about some awful stuff that happened was the catalyst that turned me down the path of Jesus about a year and a half ago now and that the healing so far had prepared us for all the stuff with Lyla.  Well shortly after it all went down and I turned back to Jesus, I was asked by an old friend (hadn't heard from him in like 18 years?) to play bass for worship during a chapel service at Mount Vernon Nazarene University's homecoming.  Literally, if he would've asked 2 weeks prior, I would've laughed at him and hung up because that's where I was with God.  However... He softened my heart to accepting Him again.  He softened my heart to worship music.  He softened my heart to Mount Vernon as I had been carrying anger and resentment toward the school for years.  God brought it all together in one event that I consider my "Musical baptism", and "Homecoming" (Yeah yeah, we played homecoming at homecoming lol) was the core song for me.  So, just by chance I get the privilege of playing this song on Easter Sunday???  

Please... I urge you to listen to the lyrics.  

And please... come worship with us on Easter Sunday.  Just.... don't look at the crazy dude that's crying over on the drums.  He might have issues. :D
