_"It's been a long December and there's reason to believe maybe this year will be better than the last..." - The Counting Crows (A Long December)_

Happy New Year!  A small handful of updates incoming.

Up until Sunday, Lyla was having about a focal every other day, and about 5-9 auras per day.  On Sunday, she had a large focal seizure that she had a lot of trouble coming out of lasting about 10 minutes total.  We called 911, but we didn't have her transported because she came out of the seizure while they were there.  Unfortunately, she wasn't able to talk for a solid hour - that's always scary for us.  The next day was a ZERO day, and only 1 aura yesterday, so we've been soaking in what we can.  

Christmas was... emotional.  So was her birthday.  I can only speak for myself with this part, but it was as if there was an element of finality with each gift she opened and each candle she blew out.  Not a certainty, but more of a question - "Is this the last Christmas gift she'll open from [insert name here]?", or "Is this the last time she'll get to choose what she'd like to do for her birthday?", or "Is this the last time she'll smile ear to ear while being sung to by a team of servers at a restaurant?"  We've been savoring every bit of it, and it was pretty heartbreaking for us once it was finished.  Her gifts to us too - she spent HOURS making her own gifts for us, and we didn't really know what she was making until we opened them up on Christmas morning.  She gave us each a handmade "booklet" thing and printed off pictures that were some of her favorite memories with each of us.  I'm sure she didn't mean it like this, but it felt like she was providing us a keepsake for when she's either gone or unable to communicate.  As if she's saying, "I don't know what's going to happen, but please remember me and remember these specific moments that I printed off for you."  I recognize that I'm skipping way ahead and going to the worst case scenario, but hey... if I'm being honest and transparent, that's just where we've been living emotionally.

We had a follow up appointment with our doctor in Cleveland after the surgical board met to discuss Lyla's case and what our next steps are.  They are about 90% certain she has Rasmussen's Encephalitis (in case you've missed it, Rasmussen's Encephalitis is a rare neurological disorder causing inflammation on one hemisphere of the brain.  This inflammation leads to progressive and permanent brain damage). The only known treatment for Rasmussen's is a hemispherectomy, which disconnects the affected hemisphere of the brain from the normal-functioning hemisphere so as to not spread to the unaffected side.  The big problem we are facing is that Lyla's dominant hemisphere (where her language is located) is the affected hemisphere.  A surgery at this point would likely be devastating to her language and language processing, along with causing right side paralysis and vision loss.

The board was unanimous in saying that they'd like to give IVIg infusions (immunotherapy) a chance to work before moving forward with any surgery due to the sheer amount of risk involved with the surgery route.  We're stuck here between a rock and a hard place because the doctors already told us to not put our hope in this treatment as it has a less than 10% chance of working.  It feels as if we are putting all our eggs in this basket that is unlikely to work. As Kayla and I are discussing, Kayla brought up her own conviction that our hope should only be in a person, Jesus. We have to keep reminding ourselves that He knows what He's doing a bit better than we do.

So next up is her first IVIg infusion early next week and then a medical trip to Mayo Clinic in Minnesota for just under a week at the beginning of February in order to get a second opinion.
