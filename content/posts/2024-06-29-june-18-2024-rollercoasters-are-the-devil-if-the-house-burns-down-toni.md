My apologies for the delay on this one.   I wrote this on June 18th and then sat on the draft for a while, but the date in the title is correct, I promise!  

Lots to update, so I'll get right into it:

**Lyla's update**:
She's been having a bit of a roller-coaster ride when it comes to her focal seizures, and it seems to (maybe?) be related to when she's super active (bouncy house, just dance, softball at times, etc.).  She's had as many as 6-7 focals in a week and as little as none.  It's mentally challenging at the moment.  Just when you let your guard down a bit, she seems to have another. 

And then, the follow up call with our Doctor. 

Thursday morning, we had a very sobering call with our doctor.  The seizure meds don't seem to be doing the trick so we're switching to a steroid and treating as though she has "[Rasmussen's Encephalitis](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4005780/#:~:text=Rasmussen)" (To be clear, we don't know if this is the case... but it IS a next logical step).  Our hearts absolutely dropped when those words were said.  The last time those words were said, it was a fraction of a possibility that that's what she was dealing with.  Seriously... it's a 1 in 5 million type of thing.  There exists a wide variety of possible outcomes if this is the case, from easy (simply taking a med for the rest of her life) to downright terrifying (paralysis, severe loss of mental/physical capacity, and one I refuse to talk about).  Our challenge at this point is to take it one day at a time and not get ahead of ourselves.  I have to double down on God here - He's truly been faithful and present through the wreckage so far... why wouldn't he put on a full display of beauty through this new challenge?  Time to start looking for it, I guess?  

**Prayer:**
God, if I've gotten lax in my dependence on You... shame on me.  I've written more than once about staying spiritually broken so as to not fall into the same pattern I've fallen into historically - approaching each day like I've got this on my own.  You give and You take away, and I'm choosing tonight to say blessed be the name of the Lord.  Just please... please don't take away her future.  She prays to be a mother someday.  She prays for her future husband.  She wants to sing at church and lead others into worship.  Those are things You can choose to use for Your glory, right?? Just please God, hear my cry!!!  Please find it in Your heart to not take that away!

**The House**:
It's stripped down to the studs at this point, so I think we're getting close to reconstruction!  

We got out of town on Friday/Saturday for Father's Day in Columbus (and heck... we kinda needed a getaway as a family after that call on Thursday).  It's kinda funny how these stories intersect and how God puts us in contact with the right people at the right times.  We were at "Pins" in Easton and JUST SO HAPPENED to run into a friend we hadn't seen in probably 2 years.  I had gone to school with Jonny from 2nd grade through graduation and we ran into his wife, Vanessa.  They are both devout Christians and we certainly look up to them in their faith.  She explained how she has read a little about our story(ies?) and it put her in mind of a Switchfoot song "If the house burns down tonight".  We listened to it on the way home, and it couldn't have been more perfect both literally and as a metaphor spiritually and what's going on with Lyla - you better believe it's the song on repeat!! :)

**Guatemala update**:
The trip was life changing.  Still super emotional about it... not done processing yet, but I'll be going back that's for sure!  Two massive takeaways so far:

1. My problems are really small.
2. I learned what it means to be a "Prayer Partner" and the two way street that exists where we are ministering to each other.  Somehow, Lyla's story made it down to Guatemala, and several people took me aside and (through broken english) made a point to communicate to me that they have been PRAYING EVERY DAY for Lyla and family.  Needless to say, the more I was told this, the more of a wreck I became emotionally.  I mean, tell me you're praying for me and I can handle myself just fine, but tell me you're praying for my daughter? whew... just... thank you.  I'm pretty sure I became known as "The Cryer" or something. ha!
